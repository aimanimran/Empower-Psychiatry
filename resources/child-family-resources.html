Mental Health Education

Child & Family Resources

Mental health resources for children, adolescents, parents, and families across Bloomingdale, Addison, and the Chicagoland area.

Supporting Children and Families

Mental health challenges can affect children, adolescents, and entire families in different ways. Emotional difficulties, behavioral concerns, academic stress, family conflict, anxiety, trauma, and mood symptoms can impact development, relationships, and daily functioning.

Early support and compassionate intervention can improve emotional resilience and help families build healthier communication patterns, coping skills, and long-term stability.

Common Concerns in Children and Adolescents

  • Anxiety, fears, or school avoidance
  • Behavioral outbursts or emotional dysregulation
  • Attention or learning difficulties
  • Depression or social withdrawal
  • Sleep disturbances
  • Family stress or communication challenges
  • Trauma-related symptoms

Family-Centered Treatment

Treatment for children and adolescents often works best when families are actively involved. Care may include therapy, medication management, behavioral support strategies, parent education, school collaboration, and structured routines that support emotional growth.

Family-centered approaches can strengthen communication, improve emotional understanding, and create a more supportive home environment. Parents and caregivers also benefit from guidance and resources as they navigate developmental or behavioral concerns.

Community and School Support

Schools, counselors, pediatricians, and mental health professionals throughout Addison, Bloomingdale, and DuPage County can provide important support systems for families. Educational accommodations, counseling services, and community-based resources may help children succeed socially, emotionally, and academically.

Trusted Organizations

  • American Academy of Child & Adolescent Psychiatry
  • SAMHSA
  • Illinois Children’s Mental Health Partnership

Local & Crisis Support

For urgent emotional support, call or text 988 for the Suicide & Crisis Lifeline. Residents in Addison, Bloomingdale, DuPage County, and the greater Chicagoland area may also benefit from local counseling centers, hospitals, and emergency mental health services.

When To Seek Help

Consider professional evaluation if symptoms are affecting work, school, sleep, relationships, emotional wellbeing, or daily functioning. Early support and treatment can improve long-term outcomes and quality of life.
